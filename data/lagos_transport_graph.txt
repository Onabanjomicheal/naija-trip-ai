# SECTION 2: TRANSPORT MODES — OVERVIEW
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

## MODE TYPES IN LAGOS

| Mode          | Operator       | Payment    | Comfort  | Reliability | Coverage         |
|---------------|----------------|------------|----------|-------------|------------------|
| BRT (Blue)    | LAGBUS/LAMATA  | Cowry Card | High     | Medium-High | Major corridors  |
| BRT (Red)     | LAGBUS         | Cowry Card | High     | Medium      | Inner city       |
| Danfo (Yellow)| Private        | Cash       | Low      | Variable    | City-wide        |
| Keke Napep    | Private        | Cash       | Medium   | High        | Local/last-mile  |
| Okada (Bike)  | Private        | Cash       | Low      | High        | Last-mile        |
| Ferry         | LASWA/Private  | Cash/Card  | High     | Low-Medium  | Waterfront zones |
| Ride-hail     | Bolt/Uber      | App        | High     | High        | City-wide        |
| Minibus       | Private        | Cash       | Low-Med  | Variable    | Suburban         |

## NOTES
- Okada is officially banned on expressways and many major roads; still operates on inner roads.
- Cowry Card is rechargeable, mandatory for BRT. Top-up at BRT terminals.
- Danfo fares are negotiable during off-peak and fixed (higher) during peak/rain.
- Keke does not operate on expressways (Lagos law).

## BRT CORRIDOR 1: IKORODU – TBS (Blue BRT)
Longest BRT route in Lagos. LAMATA/LAGBUS operated.

Direction: Ikorodu → Mile 12 → Ketu → Ojota → Maryland → Anthony → Palmgrove → Gbagada → Oworonshoki → TBS/CMS

Key Stops (in order):
  Ikorodu Terminus → Benson → Owutu → Majidun → Irawo → Mile 12 →
  Ketu → Ojota → Maryland → Anthony → Palmgrove → Gbagada Roundabout →
  Oworonshoki → Onipanu → Jibowu → Yaba → Oyingbo → CMS → TBS

Typical Fare (2026): ₦960 – ₦970 (full route)
Segment fares:
  Ikorodu → Mile 12:     ₦290
  Ikorodu → Ojota:       ₦450
  Ikorodu → Maryland:    ₦600
  Ikorodu → TBS:         ₦960
  Ojota → TBS:           ₦520
  Mile 12 → TBS:         ₦700
  Maryland → TBS:        ₦400

Peak Duration: 70 – 110 min (full route)
Off-Peak Duration: 55 – 80 min
Notes: Prone to delay at Ketu junction, Maryland underbridge, and Jibowu.

---

## BRT CORRIDOR 2: OSHODI – OBALENDE (Blue BRT)
Via Apongbon bridge or Lagos Island express.

Direction: Oshodi → Mushin → Ojuelegba → Costain → Iganmu → Ijora → Obalende

Key Stops:
  Oshodi Terminal → Mushin Roundabout → Ojuelegba → Costain → Iganmu →
  Ijora → Apongbon → Lagos Island → Obalende

Typical Fare (2026): ₦630
Peak Duration: 40 – 65 min
Off-Peak Duration: 30 – 45 min
Notes: Very high frequency in the morning. Oshodi Terminal 2 is primary boarding point.

---

## BRT CORRIDOR 3: IYANA IPAJA – OBALENDE (Blue BRT)
Serves western Lagos corridor.

Direction: Iyana Ipaja → Agege Motor Road → Oshodi → Ojuelegba → Obalende

Key Stops:
  Iyana Ipaja → Ikeja → Agege Motor Road → Oshodi →
  Mushin → Ojuelegba → Costain → Obalende

Typical Fare (2026): ₦1,090
Peak Duration: 65 – 90 min
Off-Peak Duration: 50 – 70 min
Notes: Often fastest to change at Oshodi and board another BRT.

---

## BRT CORRIDOR 4: MILE 2 – TBS / CMS (Blue BRT)
Serves Amuwo-Odofin and Apapa corridor.

Direction: Mile 2 → Orile → Costain → Ijora → TBS

Key Stops:
  Mile 2 → Orile → Iganmu → Costain → Ijora → Apongbon → TBS/CMS

Typical Fare (2026): ₦680
Peak Duration: 40 – 60 min
Off-Peak Duration: 30 – 45 min
Notes: Connects Festac/Satellite Town via Mile 2 feeder danfo.

---

## BRT CORRIDOR 5: BERGER – OSHODI (Inner City BRT)

Direction: Berger → Ojodu → Agege → Ogba → Oshodi

Key Stops:
  Berger → Ojodu-Berger → Agege → Pen Cinema → Oshodi

Typical Fare (2026): ₦580
Peak Duration: 45 – 65 min
Off-Peak Duration: 35 – 50 min
Notes: Feeds into Oshodi hub for onward island routes.

---

## BRT CORRIDOR 6: BERGER – AJAH (Long BRT)
One of the longest single BRT routes.

Direction: Berger → Maryland → Ojota → CMS → VI → Lekki → Ajah

Key Stops:
  Berger → Maryland → Palmgrove → Gbagada → CMS → Obalende →
  Five Cowrie Creek → Lekki Phase 1 → Chevron → VGC → Ajah

Typical Fare (2026): ₦1,710
Peak Duration: 90 – 130 min
Off-Peak Duration: 70 – 100 min
Notes: Subject to Third Mainland Bridge / Carter Bridge congestion. Avoid 7–10am, 4–8pm.

---

## BRT CORRIDOR 7: OSHODI – AJAH (Via Obalende)
Often done as a two-leg journey.

Leg 1: Oshodi → Obalende (₦630, ~40-55 min)
Leg 2: Obalende → Ajah (₦630–800, ~35–55 min)
Total Fare: ₦1,260 – ₦1,430
Total Time: 75 – 110 min

---

## BRT CORRIDOR 8: OBALENDE – AJAH / LEKKI

Direction: Obalende → Five Cowrie Creek Bridge → Lekki Phase 1 → Chevron → VGC → Ajah

Key Stops:
  Obalende → Falomo → Maroko → Lekki Phase 1 → Chevron →
  Jakande → VGC Junction → Ajah

Typical Fare (2026): ₦630 (Obalende–Lekki Phase 1), ₦800–900 (Obalende–Ajah)
Peak Duration: 40 – 70 min
Off-Peak Duration: 30 – 50 min
Notes: BRT and Danfo compete strongly on this corridor.

---

## BRT CORRIDOR 9: AGBARA – MILE 2 (Badagry Expressway Corridor)
Emerging route for Ojo/Badagry traffic.

Direction: Agbara → Ojo → Trade Fair → Alaba → Mile 2

Typical Fare (2026): ₦700 – ₦900 (segment-dependent)
Peak Duration: 60 – 90 min
Off-Peak Duration: 45 – 70 min
Notes: Heavy truck traffic near Alaba/Mile 2 junction.

## BRT CORRIDOR 10: IKEJA - CMS / TBS (Inner City Express)
Connects Ikeja core to Lagos Island via major hubs.

Direction: Ikeja -> Maryland -> Anthony -> Palmgrove -> Oworonshoki -> Jibowu -> Yaba -> Oyingbo -> CMS -> TBS

Key Stops:
  Ikeja Terminal -> Maryland -> Anthony Village -> Palmgrove -> Oworonshoki ->
  Jibowu -> Yaba -> Oyingbo -> CMS -> TBS

Typical Fare (2026): NGN 650 - NGN 850
Peak Duration: 60 - 95 min
Off-Peak Duration: 45 - 75 min
Notes: Watch for heavy delays at Third Mainland Bridge and Jibowu.

---

## BRT CORRIDOR 11: IKEJA - OBALENDE - VICTORIA ISLAND (Mixed Service)
Common transfer pattern from mainland to island.

Direction: Ikeja -> Maryland -> Ojota -> Oworonshoki -> CMS -> Obalende -> Victoria Island

Key Stops:
  Ikeja -> Maryland -> Ojota -> Oworonshoki -> CMS ->
  Obalende -> Falomo -> Victoria Island

Typical Fare (2026): NGN 750 - NGN 1,050
Peak Duration: 70 - 110 min
Off-Peak Duration: 50 - 85 min
Notes: Transfer often required at Obalende during peak hours.

---

## BRT CORRIDOR 12: CMS / TBS - LEKKI PHASE 1 - AJAH (Coastal)
Primary island-to-Lekki corridor with high demand.

Direction: CMS -> Obalende -> Falomo -> Lekki Phase 1 -> Jakande -> Chevron -> VGC -> Ajah

Key Stops:
  CMS -> Obalende -> Falomo -> Lekki Phase 1 -> Jakande ->
  Chevron -> VGC Junction -> Ajah

Typical Fare (2026): NGN 700 - NGN 1,000
Peak Duration: 50 - 90 min
Off-Peak Duration: 35 - 70 min
Notes: Congestion around Lekki toll corridor is common during peak.

# SECTION 5: KEKE NAPEP (TRICYCLE) ROUTES
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Keke serves last-mile and intra-community trips. Capacity: 3 passengers.
Keke does NOT operate on expressways. It covers estate roads, inner streets, and markets.
Fares are per person (shared) or full charter (solo fare = 2.5–3× per-person rate).

# SECTION 6: FERRY / WATER TRANSPORT (LASWA)
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Lagos has an active water transport network managed by LASWA and private operators.
Useful for avoiding severe road gridlock, especially Apapa/Island/VI.

## LASWA TERMINALS
- Marina Terminal (CMS/TBS area) — main hub
- Five Cowrie Creek Terminal (Lekki entrance)
- Ikorodu Ferry Terminal
- Apapa Terminal
- Ebute Metta Terminal

Notes:
- Ferries subject to weather. Rough water or heavy rain = suspension.
- Cowry Card NOT accepted; cash only at most terminals.
- Water bus (LASWA) is more structured; private boats are cheaper but riskier.

# SECTION 9: TRANSFER LOGIC (REAL LAGOS ROUTING)
# ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

## GOLDEN RULES OF LAGOS TRANSFERS

1. Mainland → Island: Almost always through **Obalende** or **CMS/TBS**.
2. Western Lagos → Island: Go through **Iyana Ipaja → Oshodi → Obalende**.
3. Eastern Lagos → Island: Go through **Ojota → Maryland → TBS** or **Berger → Gbagada → CMS**.
4. Lekki / Ajah destination: Hub through **Obalende** (most efficient).
5. Badagry / Ojo direction: Route through **Mile 2**.
6. Ikorodu → Anywhere: Start with BRT toward **Mile 12 or TBS**.
7. Airport (MMIA): Drop at **Ikeja** or **Murtala Muhammed Airport Rd**, use ride-hail for terminal access.

## RAIN EFFECTS ON LAGOS TRAFFIC
- Light rain (< 30 min): +20–40% to journey times.
- Heavy downpour (sustained): +60–150% to journey times. Some routes impassable.
- Flood-prone roads: Ago Palace Way, Iyana Iba, Jakande Estate, Alapere, Bariga, Gbagada low road.
- Danfo fares increase by 30–100% during heavy rain ("rain fare" culture).
- BRT fares are fixed but queues extend significantly.
- Ride-hail surge pricing: 2×–4× during and after heavy rain.

## DAY-OF-WEEK PATTERNS

| Day         | Pattern                                                                    |
|-------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Monday      | Extremely heavy; all corridors. Avoid island bridges before 10am.          |
| Tuesday     | Heavy morning peak; moderates by afternoon.                                |
| Wednesday   | Average; manageable if leaving before 7am or after 10am.                  |
| Thursday    | Moderate; market days in some areas (Mile 12, Balogun) create local jams. |
| Friday      | Worst day. Evening jam starts as early as 2pm. Avoid 3–10pm.              |
| Saturday    | Heavy 9am–2pm (market/social trips). Light by evening.                    |
| Sunday      | Light until church-out (11am–1pm spike). Free flow by 3pm.                |

## MARKET DAY EFFECTS
- Mile 12 Market: Monday & Thursday — heavy truck traffic, Ketu–Ojota route gridlock.
- Balogun Market: Tuesday & Friday — Carter Bridge/CMS backed up to Obalende.
- Alaba International Market: Monday, Wednesday — Mile 2 expressway backed up.
- Computer Village (Ikeja): Saturday — Allen/Computer Village area standstill.
- Ladipo Spare Parts (Mushin): Wednesday — Mushin corridor affected.

## ROUTING LOGIC PRIORITIES
1. Always validate terminal/stop names using LocationIQ, OSRM, or GTFS data first.
2. Use this graph for transfer logic, realistic fares, and natural language explanations.
3. Fares are 2026 post-increase estimates. Add ±15% margin for variation.
4. Always account for time of day when quoting journey duration.
5. During rain events, warn user of surge fares and possible flooding delays.
6. For Apapa/port-bound routes, always warn about truck traffic.

## DYNAMIC FARE TRIGGERS
- Rain: +30–100% on danfo fares; ×2–4 on ride-hail.
- Peak hour: +10–25% on danfo; BRT fares remain fixed.
- Night (after 10pm): +50–100% on keke and danfo charter.
- Public holiday: Normal or slightly lower.

## COMFORT RANKING (Low → High)
Danfo < Keke < BRT < Ride-hail / Ferry

## SPEED RANKING OFF-PEAK (Slow → Fast)
Keke < Danfo < BRT < Ride-hail (no traffic) < Ferry (water route)

## SPEED RANKING PEAK HOUR
Danfo ≈ BRT (both stuck) << Keke (shortcuts) << Ferry (unaffected) << Ride-hail (surge pricing)
